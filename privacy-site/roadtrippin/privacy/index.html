Roadtrippin

Privacy Policy

Roadtrippin is designed for passengers to record license plates and road-trip memories, including while offline. This policy explains what the app handles and the choices you have.

Effective July 20, 2026

The short version

  • Your trips remain available on your device without an account.
  • Cloud sync is optional and uses your shared Bent Nail Studio account.
  • Roadtrippin does not sell personal information or use behavioral advertising.
  • Shared results omit precise coordinates.
  • Deleting the shared account also deletes data in other apps using that account.

Information Roadtrippin handles

Trip and user content

You may provide trip names, destinations, travelers, vehicle details, notes, stops, reusable tags and quantities, license-plate sightings, dates, times, and other free-form content. This information may reveal details you choose to write about yourself or other people.

Location

With your permission, Roadtrippin records precise latitude and longitude when you log a plate, journal note, or stop. It may use the operating system’s geocoder to add a readable place name. Roadtrippin does not continuously track your route. You can deny location permission, enter or correct a place manually, and continue using the app.

Photos

You may attach photos from your camera or photo library to journal entries. Before storage, Roadtrippin removes embedded EXIF metadata and resizes the image to a maximum dimension of 2,048 pixels. Any location shown for a journal entry is stored separately from the photo.

Account information

An account is optional. If you create or use one, authentication may process your email address, password credential, verified-email status, provider identity, and basic profile information supplied by Google or Apple. Passwords are handled by the authentication provider and are not stored in Roadtrippin’s trip database.

Diagnostics

Roadtrippin uses Sentry to receive crash reports and nonfatal sync failures. Diagnostic reports may include device and operating-system details, app version, error type, and technical stack traces. Roadtrippin configures Sentry to remove email addresses, user IDs, authentication tokens, coordinates, notes, tags, photo paths, request bodies, and similar trip content. Session replay, behavioral analytics, advertising analytics, and performance tracing are disabled for this beta.

How information is used

Roadtrippin uses information only to:

  • save, display, edit, map, and share the trip records you request;
  • sync and restore your retained records when you choose cloud sync;
  • authenticate your optional account;
  • calculate achievements and progress; and
  • diagnose crashes, reliability problems, and sync failures.

Local storage, cloud sync, and service providers

The on-device database is the primary source for the app, so ordinary trip tracking works offline. If you sign in and confirm an upload, Roadtrippin copies your retained device trips and photos to Supabase. Supabase provides authentication, a protected PostgreSQL database, and private photo storage. Sentry provides the scrubbed diagnostic service described above. Google and Apple process sign-in information only when you choose those providers.

Cloud records are protected by per-user access rules. Private journal photos are stored under account- and trip-scoped paths. Roadtrippin does not place a service-role key in the app.

Sharing

Roadtrippin shares content only when you invoke the platform share sheet. A shared map image or text summary may contain trip names, plate progress, readable place names, or other content shown in the preview, but it never includes precise latitude and longitude. The apps or people you select receive the shared result under their own privacy practices.

Retention and your choices

Local trip data remains on the device until you edit it, remove it, clear local app data, uninstall the app, or complete shared-account deletion. Signing out does not erase local trips. Synced information remains in your shared account until you remove the applicable records or delete that account. Deleted cloud records and photos are removed from active systems, although limited encrypted backups and security logs may persist temporarily as required for recovery, fraud prevention, legal obligations, or platform operations.

You can review and correct sightings, locations, journal entries, photos, trip details, and other stored content in the app. You can also deny camera, photo-library, and location access in system settings.

Important

Deleting your shared account

The same Supabase identity is used by Roadtrippin and Muddlist. Roadtrippin currently supports full shared-account deletion only. This action permanently deletes:

  • your shared authentication identity;
  • all synced Roadtrippin trips, sightings, journal content, achievements, and photos;
  • all Roadtrippin data retained on the device completing deletion; and
  • your Muddlist tasks and deleted-task records associated with the same identity.

This cannot be undone and may stop you from signing in to any Bent Nail Studio app that uses that identity. To request deletion in Roadtrippin, open Settings → Account → Delete shared account, read the warning, and enter the required confirmation phrase while signed in.

If you cannot access the app, email privacy@bentnail.studio from the address associated with your account. We will verify account ownership before processing a request.

Children

Roadtrippin is a general-audience app and is not directed to children under 13. An adult or person old enough to manage an online account and consent to location access should manage optional cloud features. Please do not include another person’s sensitive information in trip notes without permission.

Security and international processing

Bent Nail Studio uses reasonable technical and organizational safeguards, but no device, transmission, or storage system is completely secure. Service providers may process information in countries other than your own, subject to their contractual safeguards and applicable law.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as Roadtrippin changes. We will post the revised effective date here and provide additional notice in the app when a change materially affects your choices.
